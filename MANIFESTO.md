## The State of Open Source in 2025–2026

Open source used to mean something. People joined projects. They built stuff together. They argued about architecture, fought over naming conventions, submitted PRs that got torn apart in review, and came back stronger. The friction is what made the Ruby community amazing. You earned your place. You understood the codebase. You cared.

Now we are turning into an NPM savagery. 847 gems doing a half-assed job because someone pointed an AI at a problem and published the first thing it vomited. Why even architect anything when you can make AI generate a sloppy version of Journey, Arel, Puma, or whatever else took real engineers years to design?

Product Hunt became a shit show. Half-assed products that are mostly AI-generated slop or stolen code with a fresh coat of paint and a landing page. Fork, rebrand, deploy, claim innovation. Ship fast, think never.

## Why I Roast People

I roast people because they show up with bad intent. That is like someone claiming to have been part of a hurricane rescue effort as military, just to find out he was Xboxing in his basement the whole time. If you fork someone's work, strip the attribution, and call it your own — you deserve to be called out. Not quietly. Loudly.

## How I Use AI

I do use AI. I am not a hypocrite.

But I do not use it to vibe-code and steal other people's work. When I find a project I like, I join it. I contribute. I earn my commits.

I use AI because I have LLI (Low Latent Inhibition), which causes me to write stuff that is either too complex or over-engineered for the mere mortal. AI helps me translate the architecture in my head into something that compiles on the first try instead of the third. It is a tool. Not a co-founder. Not an "AI engineer." A tool.
